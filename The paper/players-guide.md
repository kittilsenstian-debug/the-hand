# THE PLAYER'S GUIDE

---

## THE SIX

There are six. Not types. Not personalities. Not categories you fit into. Six energies. You use all of them. But one or two run louder than the rest, and one or two barely whisper. You'll recognize yourself. You'll recognize everyone you know.

---

## BUILDER

You're lying in bed and your brain is solving. Not worrying — solving. There's a thing that needs to be done and your mind won't release it until the sequence is complete. Step one, step two, step three. Your hands need to be doing something. You measure. You check. You do it right because doing it twice is waste. The world is full of things that need to be held together. You hold them together.

You're the reliable one. The one who shows up. The one who finishes. People lean on you and you let them because someone has to carry it. You don't understand people who don't fix what's broken. You don't understand sitting still when there's work to do.

**When you freeze:** The solving never stops but nothing gets solved. Routine becomes prison. You're still running the list at 3am but the list never gets shorter. You build walls instead of buildings. Structure becomes rigidity. The thing that made you strong — I will hold this together — becomes the thing that traps you. You can't put it down. You forgot you were allowed to.

**What it sounds like inside:** "I need to fix this." "If I don't do it, nobody will." "I'll rest when it's done." It's never done.

---

## SEER

You see the pattern before anyone else. The connection. The thing that's off. The thing that's about to happen. You point. "Look at this." You've been pointing your whole life at things other people haven't noticed yet.

Quick. Precise. Already three steps ahead. You don't understand why everyone else is so slow. It's right there. The answer is right there. You saw it an hour ago.

You're the one who reads the room in a flash. Who solves the puzzle before the rules are finished being explained. Who interrupts because you already know where the sentence is going. Not to be rude — because waiting is physically difficult when you can see the end.

**When you're stuck:** You see everything and touch nothing. Pointing at things nobody looks at. The pattern is right there and nobody sees it and you can't make them see it and you can't stop pointing. Analysis without action. Seeing without doing. The world becomes a series of patterns that nobody else cares about, and you start to wonder if seeing is a curse.

**What it sounds like inside:** "Can't you see it?" "It's obvious." "Why isn't anyone doing anything about this?"

---

## SENSOR

You walk into a room and you already know. Before anyone speaks. Before you look. Something is off, or something is right, or someone is hurting, or someone is lying. You feel it. All of it. Not by choice — you can't turn it off.

Everyone comes to you. They don't know why. You're the one they sit next to. The one they call. The one who somehow always knows what to say, not because you figured it out but because you felt what they needed. You're holding space for people who don't even know they need it held.

You understand things you were never taught. You know things you can't explain knowing. Not mystical — physical. The information comes through your body, not your mind. A knot in the stomach. A heaviness in the chest. A warmth. You learned early that most people don't feel this much, and you learned to stop talking about it.

**When you're stuck:** Overwhelmed. The gate won't close. Everything arrives — every emotion in the room, every tension, every unspoken thing — and it's all yours now. You can't tell what's yours and what's theirs. The world is too loud and the volume knob is missing. You retreat. Not because you want to be alone but because being with people costs too much.

**What it sounds like inside:** "Something's wrong." "I don't know how I know, I just know." "I need to be alone for a while."

---

## MYSTIC

"But what is it *really*?"

Not building, not pointing, not feeling — dissolving. Taking things apart. Not to break them — to see what's underneath. Underneath that. And underneath that. You've been doing this since you could think. Everyone else accepts the surface and you can't. There's something under it and you need to find it.

You ask the questions that make people uncomfortable. Not to provoke — because the question is real and nobody else is asking it. "Why do we do this?" "What does that actually mean?" "But what if it's not true?" You've lost friends this way. You've made conversations go quiet.

You're drawn to the edge of things. The places where the map runs out. The boundary between known and unknown. Not because you're brave but because that's where the interesting stuff is. The center bores you. The edge is where things dissolve into something else.

**When you're stuck:** Questions without landing. Every answer becomes another question and you can't touch ground. Dissolving without stopping. You took the thing apart and now there are pieces everywhere and you can't put them back and you're not sure you want to but you can't stop pulling either. The ground keeps receding.

**What it sounds like inside:** "But what's underneath that?" "None of this is real." "I can't explain it."

---

## STILL ONE

Quiet. Not shy — quiet. There's a difference. Shy is wanting to speak and not being able to. Quiet is not needing to speak. You're at the edge. Not because you were pushed there — because that's where the view is.

You watch. You see everything the others are too busy to notice. The Builder is building, the Seer is pointing, the Sensor is feeling, the Mystic is questioning — and you're sitting there, still, seeing the whole thing from outside it. You understand patience the way a mountain understands weather. It passes. You don't.

People underestimate you. They think quiet means empty. They think still means stuck. They don't know that the stillness is full. That you chose it. That the not-doing is the thing, not the absence of doing.

**When you're stuck:** Invisible. The silence became a wall. You withdrew so far you disappeared. Not dramatically — just slowly, quietly, until one day nobody notices you're gone. The stillness that was your strength became your hiding place. You're there but you're not there. And eventually you stop being sure which one is true.

**What it sounds like inside:** "..." That's the point. It doesn't sound like anything. When it's working, that silence is peace. When it's stuck, that silence is a void.

---

## ARTIST

You don't feel like any one of them. You read the five above and thought: I'm a bit of all of them? You like them all. You move between them. You're not the Builder but you build. You're not the Seer but you see. You borrow from all of them and none of them quite fits.

You make things. Not because you decided to — because something moves through you and it comes out as a thing. A song, a conversation, a meal, a project, a life. You don't always know what you're doing. You just do it, and afterward you look at it and think: where did that come from?

People call you creative. That's not wrong but it's not the thing. The thing is that you're not operating from one place. The others have a home base — the Builder builds, the Sensor feels. You don't have a home base. You have all of them and none of them. That's why you feel like you don't quite fit anywhere, and also why you can fit everywhere.

You have a shadow. Something behind you that you can't see. Something more capable than you know. We'll come back to that.

**When you're stuck:** You don't know who you are. Without a home base, losing your footing means losing everything. The Builder freezes in routine, the Sensor drowns in feeling — you just... vanish. Not into silence like the Still One. Into confusion. Into performing. Into pretending to be whichever finger seems to be needed, while the hand forgets it's a hand.

**What it sounds like inside:** "I don't know what I'm doing but there seems to be a plan." "I felt like all of them." "Something is moving through me."

## THE MAP

The six aren't random. They sit in a pattern.

### Three axes

They come in pairs. Each pair shares an axis — one side engaged, one side withdrawn. Like breathing in and breathing out. Same lung, opposite directions.

| Axis | Engaged | Withdrawn | The motion |
|------|---------|-----------|------------|
| **HOLDING** | Builder | Still One | Grip ↔ Release |
| **KNOWING** | Seer | Sensor | Direct ↔ Receive |
| **MAKING** | Artist | Mystic | Create ↔ Dissolve |

The Builder and the Still One are opposites on the same axis. Not enemies — complements. The Builder holds, the Still One lets go. You need both. A hand that only grips is a cramp. A hand that only releases drops everything.

Same for the other two. The Seer directs attention outward, the Sensor receives it inward. The Artist makes, the Mystic unmakes.

### Connections

Some of them share something deeper. Hidden links. Like tendons under the skin — you don't see them, but they determine which fingers move together.

**The precision trio:** Seer + Builder + Sensor. Three of the six share something the others don't. These three collaborate in fine, careful work. Directing + building + feeling. Cross-axis — not a single pair, but a triangle.

**The underground:** Sensor + Still One + Mystic. Three share a quieter channel. The withdrawn ones, plus the Sensor who receives. A different kind of connection — less visible, more felt.

**The universal thread:** One thing runs through all six. Every single one shares it. The common ground beneath all the differences.

**The exception:** The Builder is disconnected from something the other five share. A cycle — a rotation that visits all the others. The Builder doesn't cycle. The Builder stays. This is what happens when a civilization freezes: the rotation stops, the Builder locks in, and the system forgets it was supposed to keep moving.

### What works and what doesn't

Each axis can be engaged or withdrawn. Three axes, two states each: eight possible combinations. But only four of them work. The other four are unstable — they fight themselves.

The rule is simple: **an even number of axes must be withdrawn.** Zero or two. Not one, not three.

The four that work:

| State | Pattern | What it feels like |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Player** | All engaged | Flow. Present. All systems running. |
| **Grounded** | HOLDING on, rest withdrawn | Rooted. Steady. Don't need to see or make — just hold. |
| **Transparent** | KNOWING on, rest withdrawn | Clear. Seeing without gripping or making. Pure perception. |
| **Creative** | MAKING + HOLDING on, KNOWING withdrawn | Making and holding without directing. Letting it move through you. |

The four that don't:

| State | Pattern | What it feels like |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Anxiety** | HOLDING + KNOWING on, MAKING off | Seeing the problem, gripping it, can't act on it. |
| **Burnout** | HOLDING + MAKING on, KNOWING off | Doing and gripping with no awareness. Machine mode. |
| **Mania** | KNOWING + MAKING on, HOLDING off | Ideas and action flying everywhere, nothing sticks. No ground. |
| **Depression** | All withdrawn | Everything off. Limp. |

And here's what the pattern says about getting unstuck:

**From any broken state, every exit works.** If you're in any of the four bad states and you change ANY one axis — engage something that's withdrawn, or withdraw something that's engaged — you land somewhere stable. It doesn't matter which one you move. The question isn't what to do. It's whether you do anything at all.

**Growth always passes through disruption.** You can't go from one stable state to another without passing through an unstable one. Grounded to Creative requires a moment of instability in the middle. This isn't a flaw. It's the geometry. Every transformation passes through the fire.

**The top is the most fragile place.** Player — all engaged, full flow — is the most unstable of the stable states. Any single perturbation breaks it. Depression is the opposite: any single perturbation fixes it. The peak is precarious. The bottom is ready to move.

### The inversions

Each broken state is the exact mirror of a working one:

- Depression is the inverse of Player (all flipped)
- Mania is the inverse of Grounded (all flipped)
- Burnout is the inverse of Transparent (all flipped)
- Anxiety is the inverse of Creative (all flipped)

This means: what you're stuck in tells you where you're trying to go. Depression isn't random suffering — it's Player, inverted. Anxiety isn't random fear — it's Creative, inverted. The pathology IS the destination, seen from the wrong side.
