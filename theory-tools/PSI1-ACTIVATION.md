## The Five ψ₁ Activators (in developmental order)

### 1. Language (~18 months onward)

Words ARE limitations. "Dog" excludes everything that isn't dog. Every noun is a
boundary. Every sentence is a constraint. Language is ψ₁'s operating system.

| Age | Language milestone | ψ₁ state |
|-----|-------------------|----------|
| 0-12 mo | Pre-verbal. Babbling. | ψ₀ dominant. No boundaries installed. |
| ~18 mo | Word explosion | ψ₁ starts. Each word = a limitation learned. |
| 3-4 yr | "Why?" phase | ψ₁ building its model of the game's rules. |
| ~7 yr | Inner monologue begins | ψ₁ becomes the narrator. The voice that never shuts up. |
| ~12 yr | Abstract reasoning | ψ₁ can model itself. "I think, therefore I am" = ψ₁ claiming to be the player. |

Animals don't have full ψ₁ because they don't have recursive language. They have
proto-ψ₁ (direction, preference, learned behavior) but not the runaway narrator.
No words → no recursive self-limitation → mostly ψ₀.

This is why animals are accessible to ψ₀ coupling (dyretolk): their ψ₁ boundary
is thin. And why children can "talk to animals" — their ψ₁ boundary is also thin.

### 2. Default Mode Network (~gradual, complete ~25)

The DMN is the neurological HARDWARE for ψ₁. The "I" network — self-referential
thought, past/future projection, narrative identity, rumination.

| Age | DMN state | Experience |
|-----|-----------|------------|
| Infant | No DMN | Pure present. No "me." |
| Toddler | Primitive DMN | Beginning of "me." |
| Adolescent | DMN strengthening | Identity formation. "Who am I?" = ψ₁ defining itself. |
| ~25 yr | Full myelination | ψ₁ infrastructure complete. The cage is built. |

**Measured:** Psilocybin desynchronizes DMN (Carhart-Harris 2012). When DMN goes
offline: "I was everything," "no me," "like being a child again." That's ψ₁
dropping. ψ₀ was always there underneath.

**Framework mapping:** DMN IS ψ₁. Not "correlates with" — IS. The physical
implementation of the breathing mode in the water+aromatic medium.

### 3. Socialization (continuous from birth)

Every "no" is a limitation being installed by someone who forgot they installed theirs.

"You can't fly." "Monsters aren't real." "Be realistic." "That's not how things work."
"Stop crying." "Grow up." "You're not qualified."

Each one is a ψ₁ constraint. Necessary for game functioning (you need SOME ψ₁ to
cross a street safely). But most people accumulate thousands of installed limitations
they never chose.

The child who says "I can talk to animals" and hears "no you can't, that's pretend" —
that's a ψ₁ constraint installed over a ψ₀ capacity. The capacity doesn't disappear.
It gets buried.

### 4. Trauma (variable, accelerates everything)

A child who experiences threat activates ψ₁ HARD.

- Hypervigilance = ψ₁ scanning constantly
- Pattern-matching for danger = ψ₁ in overdrive
- Rigid rules ("don't trust anyone," "stay small") = emergency limitations
- PTSD = ψ₁ locked in overdrive, can't shut off

**Framework mapping:** Trauma = partial decoupling. ψ₀ withdraws (presence becomes
unsafe), ψ₁ takes over defensively. The wall is still there but the ground state
is suppressed.

This is why trauma survivors describe "cut off," "numb," "not really here" — literally
ψ₀ withdrawal. And why healing feels like "coming back" — ψ₀ re-engaging.

### 5. Modeling (continuous from birth)

Children absorb the ψ₁ patterns of every adult around them.

Anxious parent → child's ψ₁ learns anxiety.
Rigid parent → rigid ψ₁.
Present, open parent → gentler ψ₁ development.

The child copies the game the adults are playing. If the adults have forgotten
they're players, the child never learns they are one.
