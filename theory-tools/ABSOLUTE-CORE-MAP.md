### What's beneath it? Five honest questions.

**Question 1: Why POLYNOMIAL self-reference?**

The equation q + q^2 = 1 encodes self-reference as "the whole = the part + the part-of-the-part." But self-reference itself is more general than this. A system referencing itself could be encoded as a fixed-point equation f(x) = x for any f. Why f(x) = x + x^2? Why degree 2?

The framework's implicit answer: degree 2 is the MINIMUM for non-trivial self-reference. Degree 1 (f(x) = x) is trivially satisfied by everything. Degree 2 (f(x) = x + x^2) is the first equation that CONSTRAINS. And the specific form q + q^2 = 1 is the unique degree-2 fixed-point equation with:
- Exactly 2 real solutions (phi and -1/phi) = minimal duality
- A Pisot root (asymmetry = arrow of time)
- Fibonacci recursion (memory)
- Modular convergence (coupling constants)

**Status: STRUCTURAL ARGUMENT, not proof.** No theorem says "the simplest non-trivial self-referential polynomial is unique." There exist other degree-2 equations (e.g., x^2 - 2x + 1 = 0 gives x = 1, degenerate; x^2 + x - 1 = 0 gives the same phi up to sign). The specific normalization q + q^2 = 1 is forced by the nome interpretation: q must be in (0,1) for modular forms to converge, and the unique solution in (0,1) is 1/phi.

**Question 2: Why is self-reference "there" at all?**

It does not explain WHY self-reference exists. The framework treats self-reference as the irreducible given. This is honest: every theory needs an unexplained starting point. But it should be stated clearly.

**Status: PHILOSOPHICAL COMMITMENT.** The framework asserts: self-reference is prior to existence. Everything that exists is a consequence of something referring to itself. This is not derivable from anything simpler. It IS the simplest thing.
