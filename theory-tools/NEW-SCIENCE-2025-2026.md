## 1. Pines' Demon / Acoustic Plasmon

### What was found

The Pines' demon -- a quasiparticle predicted by David Pines in 1956 -- was experimentally confirmed in August 2023 in strontium ruthenate (Sr2RuO4) by Husain et al., published in Nature. The demon is a **massless, electrically neutral acoustic plasmon** arising from out-of-phase oscillations of electrons in different bands. It was detected via momentum-resolved electron energy-loss spectroscopy with velocity ~100x sound speed but ~1000x slower than surface plasmons.

No major follow-up publications from 2025-2026 were found in this search, though the original discovery team noted demons should exist in other multiband metals including iron-based and magnesium diboride superconductors.

Key properties:
- Massless and charge-neutral (does not couple to light)
- Arises from **two-band** electron systems oscillating out of phase
- Believed critical for phase transitions, superconductivity, and collective electron behavior
- Velocity intermediate between phonons and plasmons

### Relevance to framework

**Supportive (moderate).** The Pines' demon is a collective quasiparticle emerging from the interface between two electron populations -- conceptually parallel to the framework's domain wall bound states arising between two vacua. The two-band requirement echoes the Z2 structure of V(Phi). The demon's masslessness and neutrality parallel the framework's claim that domain wall excitations can be "invisible" to standard probes. Already noted in FINDINGS-v4 section 250.

### New connections

The demon exists specifically in **multiband metals with different effective masses** -- this is structurally analogous to the two vacua (phi and -1/phi) having different "weights." The fact that demons may mediate superconducting pairing (Cooper pairs) connects to the framework's claim that domain wall physics mediates coupling between domains. The iron-based superconductor connection is intriguing given the framework's iron derivation in section 252.

## 2. 40 Hz Gamma Stimulation and Alzheimer's Disease

### What was found

**Major developments in 2025.** Cognito Therapeutics presented five new poster presentations at AAIC 2025 (Alzheimer's Association International Conference, July 2025) for their Spectris device, a non-invasive 40 Hz audiovisual gamma stimulation therapy.

Key results:
- **OVERTURE study:** 69% reduction in whole-brain atrophy over 6 months vs sham. Significant preservation of white matter and myelin integrity across multiple brain regions, especially entorhinal cortex and corpus callosum.
- **Open-label extension (2 years):** 8.66 months "time saved" in activities of daily living, 9.93 months in cognitive function (MMSE), 7.48 months in whole-brain volume preservation.
- **HOPE pivotal trial (NCT05637801):** Completed enrollment of 670 participants in July 2025. Results expected **August 2026**. Regulatory submission expected end of 2026.
- **MIT March 2025:** Published expanding evidence that 40 Hz gamma stimulation promotes brain health, with data suggesting benefits may persist over years.
- **Mechanism:** White matter and myelin preservation suggest gamma stimulation maintains structural connectivity, not just neural firing patterns.

The corpus callosum finding is especially relevant -- it is the largest domain wall-like structure in the brain (boundary between hemispheres).

The HOPE trial results (August 2026) are flagged in FINDINGS-v4 as a key experimental test.
